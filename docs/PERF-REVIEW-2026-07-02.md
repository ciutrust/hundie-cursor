## 1. Executive summary — why 1000+ rows breaks, and what to fix first

### The "bad request" mechanics (verified, with arithmetic)

supabase-js sends `.eq()/.in()/.or()` filters in the **request URL** for GET, PATCH, and DELETE — only insert/upsert payloads travel in the body. Each UUID inside `.in(…)` costs ~39 URL bytes (36 chars + encoded comma). The failure zone starts around **~16KB ≈ 420 ids** (Supabase's gateway; Cloudflare hard-fails at 32KB ≈ 840 ids). A verifier reproduced the math exactly with the installed `postgrest-js` serializer. Your app crosses it in four places, all bulk actions on exactly the >1000-row flows you use weekly.

### Top fixes, in order

1. **Chunk the four unchunked `.in()` writes (S — this is your bug).**
   [proposals.ts:31](../lib/actions/proposals.ts) (`Approve all` = every high-confidence proposal id in one PATCH; >2,000 live ids ≈ 78KB URL → guaranteed 400/414), [reclassify.ts:114](../lib/actions/reclassify.ts) (select-all / find-similar bulk classify), [ai-suggestions.ts:134 & 260](../lib/actions/ai-suggestions.ts) (AI stale-mark & accept), and [proposals.ts:143-149](../lib/actions/proposals.ts) where the commit's own CHUNK=500 status update ≈ 19.5KB URL — **over the limit**, so step 2 can fail *after* step 1 already wrote classifications (partial state). The fix template already exists in your codebase: [ai-suggestions.ts:67](../lib/queries/ai-suggestions.ts) chunks at 200. Batch all URL-side `.in()`s at ~200.
2. **Paginate the commit read (S — silent data bug).** `commitApprovedProposals` selects approved proposals with **no pagination** ([proposals.ts:66-74](../lib/actions/proposals.ts)) — Supabase silently caps at 1,000 rows, so committing a >1,000-row approval **commits only the first 1,000 and reports success**. Re-clicking eventually drains it (idempotent), but nothing tells you to. Wrap in `paginateAll` + `.order("id")`.
3. **Stabilize the unstable paging (S — silent data bug, live now).** The proposals list pages >2,000 rows ordered only by non-unique `vendor_key` ([proposals.ts:90](../lib/queries/proposals.ts)) — rows can be skipped/duplicated across pages *today*; the income report pages ~6,600 rows with **no ORDER BY at all** ([income.ts:46-58](../lib/queries/income.ts)) feeding CPA-facing totals; `listCurrentAiSuggestionTransactionIds` same ([ai-suggestions.ts:39-55](../lib/queries/ai-suggestions.ts)). One-line `.order("id")` tiebreakers (income.ts should just migrate to `fetchPeriodTransactions`, finishing OPT-08).
4. **Fix the two live errors you're seeing in logs (S).** The `400` is [/categories building `entity_id=eq.<empty>`](../app/categories/page.tsx) when the entity list resolves empty, with the error swallowed (`const { data }`); the `406` is `.single()` on the `personal` entity lookup returning 0 rows under a stale session ([ai-suggestions.ts:13](../lib/queries/ai-suggestions.ts)) — swallowed too. Guard the empty entity, check errors, use `.maybeSingle()`, and add `/categories` to the auth matcher (also finding S5 in the security review).
5. **Stop paying for everything twice (M — the latency story).** Every classify click triggers `revalidatePath` **and** `router.refresh()` → two full RSC renders ≈ 60–80 backend calls per click ([transaction-list.tsx:229](../components/review/transaction-list.tsx), confirmed 3–0); every render re-scans the **entire YTD ledger just to draw sidebar badges** ([entity-home.ts:193](../lib/queries/entity-home.ts) via AppShellWrapper in all 6 layouts, confirmed 3–0 twice); the inline-suggestion effect fires ~100–150 queries per Classify render and re-fires after every click (50 of them byte-identical). Fixes: drop the redundant `router.refresh()`, replace the sidebar scan with grouped HEAD counts, hoist the token-independent `suggestion_events` query out of the 50-vendor loop (50→1).

**Workaround until fixes land:** keep bulk selections under ~400 rows (filter by confidence/search before "Approve all"), and after a big Commit, **click Commit again until it reports 0** — that drains past the silent 1,000-row cap.
